Case Study

QuantBox Research & Simulation Platform

Rust/Python infrastructure for ingestion, replay, simulation, strategy evaluation, runtime workflows, evidence capture, artifact tracking, and reproducible research.

Problem

Research and runtime workflows needed to be comparable across historical replay, simulation, and live-style execution. Results needed to be inspectable enough to debug strategy behavior and reproducible enough to support serious iteration.

What I Built

  • Rust/Python components for market-data ingestion, replay, and simulation.
  • Strategy runtime workflows with evidence capture and artifact tracking.
  • Evaluation paths for fixed-start, walk-forward, and robustness-oriented research.
  • Policy simulation and bridge work connecting research outputs to runtime behavior.
  • Portfolio sizing and account-level control concepts for operational use.

Technical Challenges

  • Keeping live, replay, and simulation behavior aligned enough to compare.
  • Capturing enough evidence to explain why a strategy or runtime path behaved a certain way.
  • Managing experiment metadata and artifacts without turning research into manual bookkeeping.
  • Balancing performance, correctness, and iteration speed across Rust and Python components.

What This Demonstrates

Systems architecture, Rust/Python engineering, simulation design, replayable workflows, evidence-based debugging, runtime thinking, and senior-level ownership of complex technical systems.
